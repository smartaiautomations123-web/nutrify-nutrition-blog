The best **high protein fast food options** in the UK aren't hiding — they're sitting on every major menu, just buried under chips and buns. Whether you're between meetings, post-gym, or stuck at a motorway services, you can comfortably clear **30-50g of protein** from a single order at almost any chain if you know what to ask for.

Below: the top picks at nine popular UK chains, approximate protein per serving, and a smart swap for each to push the number higher. Menus and figures vary — check the chain's app for the latest numbers before you order.

## Why protein matters when you're eating out

Protein is the macronutrient most likely to be **under-ordered** at fast food restaurants because chains build menus around cheap carbs and fat — buns, chips, sugary drinks. That's where margins live. The result: most "value meals" deliver 600-900 kcal but only 20-25g of protein, leaving you hungry an hour later.

A meal hitting **0.4-0.5g of protein per kg of bodyweight** (roughly 30-40g for most adults) does three useful things: it triggers **muscle protein synthesis**, blunts the post-meal blood sugar swing, and produces noticeably stronger satiety than a carb-heavy equivalent. Protein has a **thermic effect of around 20-30%**, meaning your body burns roughly a quarter of its calories just digesting it — far more than carbs (5-10%) or fat (0-3%).

The trick at fast food chains isn't avoiding them. It's **picking the chicken, fish, beef or beans option** and engineering the sides.

## The best high protein picks at UK chains

### Nando's — the easy win

Nando's is the UK's most protein-friendly chain by a mile. Almost everything starts with grilled chicken.

- **Whole chicken** — around **110g protein** for the bird. Split between two or eat half with a side and box the rest.
- **Double chicken breast fillet butterfly** — roughly **60-65g protein**. A clean, low-fat hit.
- **Sunset burger with extra chicken** — around **45-50g protein** when you add a second fillet.

**Smart swap:** sub chips for **macho peas, mixed leaf salad, or supergrains**. You'll add 8-10g of plant protein and lose 200+ kcal of refined carbs.

### Subway — the customisation king

Subway lives or dies by how you build it. Default footlongs are protein-mediocre; loaded ones are excellent.

- **Footlong Steak & Cheese with double meat** — around **50g protein**.
- **Footlong Chicken Tikka with double meat** — around **48g protein**.
- **Protein Bowl (any filling, no bread, double meat)** — **45-55g protein** depending on choice.

**Smart swap:** ask for **double meat** (usually £1-£2). It nearly doubles the protein without doubling the calories, because the bread is doing most of the kcal lifting.

### McDonald's — better than its reputation

- **Double Quarter Pounder with Cheese** — around **48g protein**.
- **Chicken Legend (plain) plus a 6-piece Chicken Selects** — around **52g protein** combined.
- **Grilled Chicken Wrap (no mayo)** — around **27g protein**, low-cal at ~350 kcal.

**Smart swap:** drop the chips and add a **second grilled chicken patty** or a side of **Chicken Selects**. You'll pay £2-£3 more for roughly 25g of extra protein and 300 fewer kcal.

### KFC — pick the bone-in

KFC's protein numbers are deceptively good when you skip the burgers and order the actual chicken.

- **3-piece Original Recipe chicken (breast + 2 pieces)** — around **55-60g protein**.
- **Mighty Bucket for One (5 pieces)** — around **65g protein**, but pick mostly breast portions.
- **Fillet Burger plus a 3-piece Hot Wings** — around **45g protein**.

**Smart swap:** swap fries for **corn on the cob and beans**. The beans add 10g of plant protein and a stack of fibre.

### Greggs — yes, really

Greggs gets a bad reputation it doesn't fully deserve. The chicken range is genuinely strong.

- **Chicken Bake** — around **18g protein** (pair two for 36g).
- **Southern Fried Chicken Baguette** — around **30g protein**.
- **Chicken & Bacon Salad Bowl** — around **28g protein** at under 350 kcal.

**Smart swap:** skip the sausage roll and grab a **chicken bake plus a high-protein yoghurt pot**. You'll clear 30g without breaking £6.

### Pret A Manger — the office worker's friend

- **Chicken Caesar & Bacon Baguette** — around **38g protein**.
- **Super Chicken Salad** — around **35g protein** with about 11g of fibre.
- **Posh Cheddar & Pickle on Artisan Baguette plus a Falafel Mezze Pot** — around **32g protein** vegetarian.

**Smart swap:** add a **boiled egg pot** (around **12g protein**) to any sandwich. It's the cheapest protein bump on the menu.

### Pizza Express — order the starter as a side

Pizza alone is rarely protein-dense. Build around it.

- **Pollo Forza pizza** — around **35-40g protein** (chicken, pepperoni and roquito peppers).
- **American Hot, leggera version** — around **30g protein** with the hole-in-the-middle salad.
- **Calabrese pizza plus a side of grilled chicken** — around **45g protein**.

**Smart swap:** order a **leggera-base pizza** (lower-cal hollow base filled with salad) and add **grilled chicken on top** for £2-£3. You'll hit 40g+ without 1000 kcal.

### Wagamama — the protein bowl gold mine

Wagamama's bowls are some of the most protein-dense fast food in the UK.

- **Chicken katsu curry with steamed rice** — around **45g protein**.
- **Teriyaki chicken donburi** — around **52g protein**.
- **Grilled tuna with steamed greens (off-menu request: brown rice + edamame)** — around **48g protein**.

**Smart swap:** ask for **extra chicken or tofu** on any bowl (usually around £3.50). Wagamama's portion bumps are generous — often adding 20g+ of protein.

### Burger King — the double-up trick

- **Double Whopper** — around **48g protein**.
- **Bacon Double Cheeseburger plus 8-piece Chicken Nuggets** — around **45g protein**.
- **Chicken Royale with extra patty** — around **40g protein**.

**Smart swap:** **swap chips for a side salad** and use the saved kcal headroom to **add a second beef patty**. Net result: similar calories, +20g protein.

## Common mistakes that tank your protein numbers

- **Defaulting to the meal deal.** Chains push meal deals because carbs are cheap. You almost always do better ordering à la carte and adding a second protein.
- **Trusting "salad" wording.** A "Caesar salad" with one chicken breast is around 25g protein; a "crispy chicken salad" with mayo can be more calorie-dense than the burger.
- **Forgetting drinks count.** A milk-based coffee adds 8-10g protein. A sugary soft drink adds 200 wasted kcal and zero protein.
- **Picking battered or breaded options by default.** Breading dilutes protein per kcal. Grilled chicken at Nando's gives roughly **double the protein per calorie** of breaded chicken at the same chain.
- **Skipping the app.** Most UK chains publish full nutrition data in their apps. The 30 seconds it takes to check usually saves 20g of protein.

## How to build a 40g+ protein meal anywhere

A reliable framework that works at any chain in the UK:

1. **Pick your protein anchor.** Grilled or roasted meat, fish, eggs, beans, tofu or paneer. Aim for a portion roughly the size of your palm — that's about 25-30g of protein on its own.
2. **Double it where you can.** Most chains let you add a second chicken breast, fillet, patty or scoop of tofu for £2-£4. This is the single best high-protein lever on any menu.
3. **Pick a smart carb.** Rice, wrap, sourdough, beans, or a single bun — not chips. Beans count twice because they bring 5-10g of plant protein.
4. **Add a dairy or egg side.** Boiled egg pot, Greek yoghurt, a flat white, or a cheese topping nudges you another 8-12g of protein.
5. **Skip the sugar drink.** Water, sparkling, or a milky coffee. Sugar drinks are pure opportunity cost.

Total: roughly **40-55g of protein**, achievable at any of the chains above for £8-£14.

## FAQ

**What fast food has the most protein in the UK?**
Bone-in chicken from Nando's and KFC, plus Wagamama donburi bowls, top the list. A whole Nando's chicken delivers around **110g of protein**, and a Wagamama teriyaki chicken donburi clears **50g** in one bowl.

**Which UK chain has the highest-protein single meal?**
Nando's, by a margin — a half chicken with macho peas and supergrains comfortably hits **65-70g of protein** for around £14. Wagamama and KFC bone-in buckets are close behind.

**Can fast food fit a high-protein diet?**
Yes, easily. Most adults need **1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of bodyweight per day** for muscle maintenance or fat loss while training. A single well-chosen fast food meal at any of the chains above can cover **a third to a half of that** in one sitting.

**Is grilled or fried chicken better for protein?**
Grilled wins on **protein per calorie**. Fried chicken still delivers meaningful protein, but the breading and oil roughly double the kcal per gram of protein. If you're tracking, default to grilled where the chain offers both.

**What's the best high-protein vegetarian fast food option?**
A Wagamama yasai katsu curry with extra tofu (around **30g protein**), a loaded Subway veggie patty footlong with double cheese (around **32g**), or a Pret falafel mezze plus boiled egg pot (around **28g**). Beans, tofu and dairy do the heavy lifting.

## The bottom line

High protein fast food is mostly a **menu literacy problem**, not a supply problem — every major UK chain has options that hit 30-50g of protein in a single order once you know to **double the protein, skip the chips, and add a dairy or egg side**. Build the meal around the protein anchor, not the bun.
